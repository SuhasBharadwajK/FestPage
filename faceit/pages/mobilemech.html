Robotics Competition
Rules:
  • There will be a total of three rounds:
    1. Qualfifying Round
    2. Semifinal Round
    3. Final Round
  • There is no limit on the number of teams.
  • Maximum size of a team is limited to 2.
  • Registration fee per team is as decided by the committee.
  • The rules and regulations are different in each round.
  • The team that qualifies the current round goes to the next round.
  • The judges' decision is final. No arguments will be entertained.
QUALIFYING ROUND
WHEELED LINE FOLLOWING ROBOT
  • Everyone has to use a 9V battery only.
  • Every robot has to move in the given path only.
  • The robot reaching start to the end will be qualified for the next round.
  • The robot has to stop at the ending point and should not enter into others' path.
  • Everyone has to bring their own kit.
SEMIFINAL ROUND
WHEELED OBSTACLE AVOIDANCE ROBOT
  • The robot reaching STOP point in least time will be declared as winner.
  • The robot may hit or touch the walls, but it should not touch or hit boundary
  • Any type of sensor can be used.
FINAL ROUND
  • Robot can take any path to reach stop point from start point.
  • The robot reaching stop point in least time will be declared as winner.
Requirments:
  • FLEXIES WHITE - 2 (BIG SIZE)
  • BLACK PERMANENT MARKERS - 2
  • BLACK COLOURED TAPE – 2 ( BIG SIZE )

Technical Quiz
Rules:
  • Each team should contain 5 members (two students from 2nd year, two students from 3rd year and one student from 4th year).
  • Form the groups yourself according to the above rule.
  • 4th year student will be the group leader.
  • Prelims will be conducted depending on the number of batches.
  • A total of four rounds will be conducted.
  • The decision of the quiz-master will be final and will not be subjected to any change.
  • The participants shall not be allowed to use mobile or other electronic instruments.
  • The questions shall be in the form of multiple choice, True / False statement, Specific-answer question etc.
  • Audience shall not give any hints or clues to the competitors.
  • Replacement of any participant of a team is not allowed after registration.
  • Teams selected for the final rounds will be allowed to give themselves an appropriate name related to the competition by which they may want to be known.
ELIMINATION ROUND:
  • This will be held in case more than six teams register for the event.
  • Each team would be given a set of question paper containing 25 multiple choice objective type questions
  • Time limit for this round 10 minutes.
  • Six teams will be selected for the STAGE ROUNDS.
  • In case of tie between 2 or more teams, 5 more questions would be asked for final selection.
  • The selected teams shall have to appear for the final round.
--STAGE ROUNDS--
General Round
  • A question will be asked to a team and if they are unable to answer it will be passed to the next team.
  • There will be 4 rounds and 24 questions.
  • Each team would be asked 4 questions each. ( Objective questions with options).
  • 10 marks for correct answer and 5 negative marks for wrong answer.
  • If a team cannot answer the question, then the question would be forwarded to the next team only.
  • Answering time is only 30 seconds.
  • Team discussion is allowed.
Rapid Fire Round
  • Each team will be asked 10 questions one after another in a time of one minute: 60 questions
  • 10 marks for the correct answer and negative 5 marks for the wrong answer. – No forwarding.
  • Answering time is only 3 seconds.
  • Team discussion is allowed.
Picture Identification Round
  • Teams will be shown pictures and will have to answer based on them.
  • There will be 2 rounds and 12 questions
  • 10 marks for the correct answer and negative 5 marks for the wrong answer.
  • Each team will be asked 2 picture identify questions. ( 1 slide in each round )
  • Answering time is only 30 seconds.
  • No passing to the next team.
  • Audience can answer in this round.
Buzzer Round
  • A question will be fired and the team that presses the buzzer first gets to answer first.
  • There will be six questions.
  • 10 marks for the correct answer and negative 5 marks for the wrong or if team doesn’t answer after they press Buzzers. – No forwarding
  • Answering time is only 30 seconds.
  • If no team presses the buzzer, that question will be cancelled.
  • Note: There will be a surprise round in case of a tie in the final round.

Make In India - Debate
Rules:
  • The topic, of course, is Make in India.
  • The participants are divided into two teams each consisting of two or three speakers.
  • The teams are allowed to choose either for or against the topic.
  • Participants must observe parliamentary language (i.e No verbal abuse aganist other partcipants).
  • No new constructive arguments may be introduced in the rebuttal period. The affirmative must, if possible, reply to the major negative arguments before the last rebuttal.
  • The judge must base his decision entirely on the material presented, without regard for other material which he may happen to possess.
  • Any gains made outside of the established procedure are disallowed.
  • The whole debate session should be completed wihin 10 mins.
  • The winner will be given a certificate of merit and a cash prize of Rs. 2000/-.
  • The runner-up will be given a certificate of merit and a cash prize of Rs. 1000/-.
